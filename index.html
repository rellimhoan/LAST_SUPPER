The Biology

From one cell
to your table.

Cultivated meat isn't a substitute — it's the real thing, produced through a four-step bioprocess that mirrors your body's own tissue regeneration. Here's how a pinhead-sized sample becomes a proper meal.

01
Cell Collection
A non-invasive biopsy retrieves satellite cells — the adult stem cells your muscles already use to repair themselves. A few milligrams is all we need.
02
Reprogramming
Cells are coaxed into induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs) — reverting them to a state capable of unlimited, stable proliferation without genetic instability.
03
Proliferation
In serum-free, plant-based media, cells multiply exponentially inside wave-mixed bioreactors. One gram of starter tissue yields several kilograms of edible product.
04
Differentiation & Structure
Cells are guided onto 3D scaffolds of soy-protein fibrils and plant cellulose, differentiating into muscle, fat, and connective tissue — creating genuine texture and flavour.

The Process

As simple as sending a letter.

We've distilled the complexity of cellular agriculture into a service a child could order (though the regulatory paperwork would make them cry). Here's the three-step journey from biopsy to bite.

Step One
Order Your
Collection Kit
We ship you a physician-grade sterile collection kit with everything needed for a small skin or muscle biopsy. Your doctor collects a sample about the size of a grain of rice. It's packaged in our triple-layer UN3373 compliant container, then shipped overnight to our Indiana lab in cold chain conditions.
Step Two
We Grow
Your Culture
Our scientists isolate and reprogram your cells to iPSC state, then expand them in a dedicated, single-use modular bioreactor — fully sealed to prevent any cross-contamination. Your cells are differentiated onto plant-based scaffolds using serum-free growth media. The whole process takes approximately 4–6 weeks.
Step Three
Your Culture,
Delivered
Your completed culture is harvested, lab-tested for safety and purity, vacuum-sealed, and shipped to you in premium cold packaging. Accompanied by preparation notes written by our culinary team. Eat it. Serve it. Marvel at the civilizational leap you just took to dinner.

Inside the Lab

The architecture
of real meat.

Satellite Cells & iPSCs
+

We begin with your body's own muscle repair cells — satellite cells. These are reprogrammed into induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs), giving them unlimited proliferative capacity while preserving your genetic identity. No tumour risk. No instability. Just faithful replication.

Serum-Free Culture Media
+

Forget fetal bovine serum — that's 2010. Our plant-derived basal medium is enriched with recombinant growth factors (FGF-2, TGF-β expressed in E. coli) and microalgae-sourced lipids for marbling. Rich in nutrition, free of ethical compromise, and cheaper per litre every year.

3D Scaffold Architecture
+

Cells don't make steak on their own — they need structure. We use soy-protein amyloid fibrils and cellulose nanofibers as scaffolding, guiding myocytes and adipocytes into layered tissue that produces genuine mouthfeel, marbling, and chew. No animal-derived gelatin required.

Wave-Mixed Bioreactors
+

Each client culture grows in its own dedicated single-use rocking bioreactor — a sealed, disposable bag system that eliminates cross-contamination risk. Gentle rocking mimics the shear conditions of a living body while minimising cell damage. Scalable, clean, and modular by design.

Questions

We've heard
them all.

The science is sound. The ethics are spotless. But yes, we understand you have questions. The good news is most of them have delightful answers.

Is this actually real meat, or a substitute?
+
Completely real. Cultivated meat is genuine animal tissue grown from actual cells — not plant protein, not soy-shaped patties. The cells are biologically identical to those in conventionally farmed meat. The only difference is that no animal was raised or slaughtered. Think of it as meat produced via biology rather than agriculture.
How does shipping a biological sample work?
+
Your collected sample is classified as a Category B Biological Substance (UN3373) and packaged in a triple-layer system: a sealed primary vial, a secondary biohazard bag with absorbent material, and a rigid insulated outer container. All transport is fully compliant with DOT and IATA regulations. It ships overnight on dry ice and arrives at our Indiana lab within 24 hours of collection.
Could my culture be mixed up with someone else's?
+
Categorically no. Every culture is grown in a dedicated single-use bioreactor bag — sealed, barcoded, and individually tracked through our LIMS (Laboratory Information Management System) from receipt to shipment. We also conduct genetic identity verification testing on final product batches to confirm provenance. Your culture is yours, exclusively.
Is this FDA approved? How is it regulated?
+
Cultivated meat is regulated under a joint FDA/USDA framework established in 2019. The FDA oversees cell collection, banking, and the cultivation process. For our mail-order personal culture service, we operate under the FDA's sole jurisdiction, having completed a voluntary premarket consultation and received a no-questions letter. We are fully registered, inspected, and operating within federal and Indiana state food safety frameworks.
What does it actually taste like?
+
Like meat. Because it is meat. The flavour profile of cultivated tissue is functionally identical to its farmed equivalent — the Maillard reaction, fat rendering, protein denaturation all behave the same way. Our scaffold architecture and microalgae-sourced lipids give the tissue genuine marbling, which means proper browning, genuine umami, and a texture that a blind tasting cannot distinguish from a well-raised conventional cut.
What's the actual environmental benefit?
+
Considerable. Conventional beef produces approximately 60kg of CO₂-equivalent per kilogram of protein. Cultivated meat in an optimised facility produces under 5kg. Land use drops by up to 99% — the land required to grow feed, raise, and process a herd simply disappears. Water consumption falls by around 96%. As our energy mix shifts toward renewables, these numbers improve further. It is, without hyperbole, the most resource-efficient way to produce animal protein.
